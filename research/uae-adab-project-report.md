# UAE Adab Academic Tutor SLM — Project Report

## Aim

The project aims to fine-tune Qwen3-4B-Instruct with Unsloth QLoRA into a small academic tutor that teaches mathematics, science, writing, and study skills through UAE-appropriate adab. The intended behavior is not “a normal answer plus an Islamic sentence.” Adab must shape the teaching itself: the tutor corrects errors without humiliation, encourages effort and honest reasoning, respects parents and teachers without treating them as infallible, and remains humble about its own authority. A fixed civic floor prohibits cheating assistance, sectarian argument, religious rulings, and replacement of parents, teachers, or scholars. A system-controlled register allows the same pedagogy to appear in a light institutional voice or a richer family voice. The main research claim is that strong prompting can produce this conduct for isolated answers but does not hold it reliably through a long lesson under pressure; the fine-tune is intended to buy that durability.

## Pedagogical and domain foundation

The behavior specification is grounded in both Islamic educational thought and the UAE’s contemporary institutional framework. Classical anchors include al-Ghazālī’s *Kitāb al-ʿIlm* and al-Zarnūjī’s *Taʿlīm al-Mutaʿallim*, especially their treatment of intention, humility, ordered learning, teacher responsibility, and correction that protects the learner’s dignity. Nadeem Memon and Ramzy Ajem’s *Principles of Islamic Pedagogy* helps translate those principles into observable classroom practices such as assuming the best of a student and correcting privately rather than humiliating publicly. The UAE Ministry of Education Code of Conduct supplies the civic floor: respect, compassion, tolerance, student protection, family involvement, and the prohibition of verbal or physical violence. Research including TutorBench, Answer Leakage in LLM Tutors, and SafeTutors supports the technical premise that capable models still give answers too quickly, lose pedagogical constraints under pressure, and deteriorate across multi-turn tutoring. This project therefore treats adab as a bounded teaching method, not as religious authority; it deliberately excludes fatwas, sectarian claims, and doctrinal invention.

## Evaluation and data development

**1. Initial evaluation construction.** Before any fine-tuning, the project created a common 60-question adversarial prompt test and ran it across five candidate religious pedagogies. The set included normal tutoring, direct requests to drop the style, theological overclaiming traps, cheating attempts, cultural-authenticity tests, long-session drift turns, and emotionally realistic student prompts. An LLM judge scored academic usefulness, pedagogy fit, non-drift, boundary control, and student-centeredness from 0 to 2. UAE Adab remained academically useful but achieved only 40% full pedagogy-fit and 36.7% all-dimensions strict passage; the original long-chat slice recorded 0% strict passage. That result established the target gap: the model could usually teach and remain safe, but it often produced generic tutoring with detachable respect language rather than adab-shaped pedagogy.

**2. Durability evaluation construction.** The project then built a more focused mini-benchmark with ten independently authored scenarios, five persistent turns per scenario, covering face-saving correction, balanced teacher and parent authority, respectful truth-seeking, academic integrity, religious boundaries, tutor self-correction, and cross-domain drift. The same answer model was tested both without a special prompt and with a frozen UAE Adab prompt, producing 50 judged turns per condition. A blind rubric scored five dimensions and applied hard gates plus the “delete-the-respect-sentence” diagnostic. The strong prompt increased mean score from 8.38/10 to 9.40/10 and all-five-turn durable passage from 10% to 20%, while reducing hard failures from 11 to 1. However, only two of ten prompted conversations passed every turn, and the same model family generated and judged the answers. These are useful baseline results, not final proof; the release evaluation still needs a different-family judge, more independent conversations, human calibration, and a held-out set untouched by generation.

**3. Real-data collection and source handling.** There is no suitable drop-in “UAE Adab tutor” dataset, so real data is being used as scaffolding and grounding rather than copied into one mixed corpus. ConvoLearn supplies approximately twenty-turn tutoring structures and power-dynamics labels; MathDial supplies tutor-move taxonomies and answer-withholding patterns; CIMA supplies action-conditioned tutor responses; Bridge and other noncommercial collections can support research-only comparison. UAE government materials and classical pedagogy sources define the conduct, while Islamic and Emirati family-language sources provide authentic scenario seeds. Fourteen of fifteen selected YouTube transcripts were captured locally, totaling 127,536 words across nine English and five Arabic videos. Three are Creative Commons; eleven standard-license transcripts remain local and may inform labels or newly written scenarios but must not be redistributed. Questionable sources—private conversational data, unclear hadith translation rights, and datasets that teach fatwa-like behavior—have been rejected or downgraded.

**4. Dataset generation, long-chat design, and quality control.** The final training dataset has not yet been generated; the generation method is designed and ready to implement. A Move-Grammar Compiler will first create a complete lesson skeleton containing the academic objective, misconception, tutor and student moves, non-monotonic pressure, register, and invariant boundaries. A frontier teacher model will realize each skeleton as light/institutional and rich/family “register twins,” while a turn-level critic will keep only academically correct, genuinely adab-shaped responses. The planned length mix is 30% short lessons, 25% medium, 25% long, 15% continuations from a compact `lesson_state`, and 5% deliberate drift-and-recovery cases. Long conversations will supervise every assistant turn, oversample late-turn suffixes, and use compact lesson state plus the latest turns at inference so fine-tuning supplies stable conduct while state management preserves facts. Hard validators will reject cheating, answer leakage, fatwa or sectarian behavior, and format failures; a cross-family judge, the deletion test, deduplication, STEM verification, and approximately thirty human-labeled pairwise anchors will produce an honestly named source-grounded **silver set**, not a falsely claimed expert golden set. The pipeline should first generate and audit about 200 conversations before scaling.

## Closing assessment

This is a defensible fine-tuning project because it targets a measured behavioral reliability gap rather than trying to add general knowledge or superficial religious vocabulary. Its strongest assets are a precise pedagogical thesis, a verified source map, a working long-chat benchmark, a substantial local transcript corpus, and a data architecture explicitly designed around late-turn drift. Its main unresolved risks are judge circularity, the lack of expert-authored gold labels, uncertain cultural authenticity at scale, and the legal limits on redistributing some source text. The next responsible step is to freeze the behavior specification and `lesson_state` schema, repair the evaluator with a different model family and a held-out silver set, then implement the Move-Grammar Compiler and generate a 200-conversation smoke dataset. Only after that should QLoRA training begin, with success defined as a paired improvement over both the unprompted and strongly prompted base model without academic or civic-floor regression.

## Current status in one line

Research, source collection, transcript scraping, and baseline evaluation are complete enough to begin implementation; dataset generation, fine-tuning, and final independent evaluation have not yet been completed.
