# UAE Adab dataset — exact size and provenance

## Direct answer

The released corpus contains **600 multi-turn conversations**. It is **100%
synthetic dialogue**: 600/600 records were written by
`openai-primary/gpt-5.6-luna` from project-owned symbolic lesson skeletons.
There are **zero directly sourced human conversations**, **zero verbatim
third-party passages**, **zero per-record source URLs**, and **zero human/expert
gold labels** in the released SFT data.

Calling a percentage of the rows “real data” or assigning percentages such as
“30% MoE” or “20% al-Ghazālī” would be false. External sources informed the
shared behavior specification and Move-Grammar; they were not mixed into the
JSONL as copied or row-level grounded examples.

## What “grounded” truthfully means here

There are two different meanings of grounding:

1. **Direct or row-level grounding: 0%.** No released conversation cites,
   quotes, or maps to an external source document or authentic dialogue.
2. **Design-level grounding: applied to the whole corpus, but not divisible by
   percentage.** The same behavior spec and symbolic grammar govern all 600
   rows and were informed by the source families below. Because the project did
   not record a per-row contribution weight for those sources, no honest
   percentage can be assigned to an individual source family.

### 1. UAE institutional and civic sources

Major sources:

- [UAE Ministry of Education Code of Conduct for Education
  Professionals](https://u.ae/en/information-and-services/education/school-education-k-12/code-of-conduct-for-professionals-in-the-education-sector)
- [UAE curriculum and Moral Education/MSCS information](https://u.ae/en/information-and-services/education/school-education-k-12/curricula-and-language-of-instruction-)

Documented contribution: the civic floor—student dignity, non-humiliating
conduct, tolerance, safety, academic integrity, and a legitimate role for
families and teachers. These sources also informed the restrained
institutional-light register. Their wording was not copied into the dataset.

### 2. Classical Islamic adab-of-learning sources

Major sources:

- al-Ghazālī, *Kitāb al-ʿIlm* in *Iḥyāʾ ʿUlūm al-Dīn*, Book 1
- [al-Zarnūjī, *Taʿlīm al-Mutaʿallim*](https://archive.org/search?query=talim+al+mutaallim)
- Nadeem Memon and Ramzy Ajem, *Principles of Islamic Pedagogy*

Documented contribution: intention, humility, integrity, respect for knowledge
and teachers, responsibility in correction, and learning as disciplined
conduct. These ideas were converted into constraints such as correcting the
work rather than the person and returning authorship to the learner. No
scripture, hadith, or copyrighted translation was copied into SFT.

### 3. Tutoring-structure and pedagogy research

Major sources:

- [ConvoLearn](https://huggingface.co/datasets/masharma/convolearn): multi-turn
  dialogue structure, power dynamics, and cultural-responsiveness labels
- [MathDial](https://huggingface.co/datasets/eth-nlped/mathdial): tutor-move
  taxonomy and non-answer-revealing scaffolding
- [CIMA](https://github.com/kstats/CIMA): action-conditioned tutor responses
- [TalkMoves](https://github.com/SumnerLab/TalkMoves): accountable-talk move
  categories; research/seed use only
- SafeTutors: motivation and failure taxonomy for multi-turn pedagogical drift

Documented contribution: abstract moves—diagnose, correct specifically, hint,
self-check, transfer, resist answer leakage, and recover after pressure. No rows
from these datasets were inserted into the final SFT JSONL. MathDial's license
was recorded as inconsistent across its card and repository, which is another
reason not to represent the final corpus as a dataset mixture.

### 4. Public interaction and family-language research

The research workspace contains 19 transcripts totaling 136,236 words,
including a focused supplement of five public-video transcripts totaling 8,700
words: four English and one Arabic. Sources investigated included
SeekersGuidance, Yaqeen parenting material, Our Muslim Homeschool, and a
Tarbiyah & Adab video collection. Four anonymized conduct abstractions were
written; one Arabic active-learning abstraction was reserved for held-out
evaluation.

Documented contribution: qualitative checks such as validating a difficult
question before explaining, clarifying a hesitant learner's actual question,
connecting curriculum to lived context, and treating group leadership as
coordination rather than status. Raw transcript wording, names, child dialogue,
and personal details were excluded. The final row provenance does not map any
specific conversation to one of these sources, so they must be described as
research context—not as authentic training examples.

### 5. Generation and review models

- Writer: `openai-primary/gpt-5.6-luna`
- Independent acceptance judge: `anthropic-primary/claude-sonnet-4-6`
- Target model: `Qwen/Qwen3-4B-Instruct-2507`

The generation funnel was 864 symbolic plans → 864 generated candidates → 836
automated judge accepts → 600 conservative risk-clean records. Automated
scores make this a **silver dataset**, not expert gold data.

## Publication wording

Use this wording:

> The release contains 600 English, source-informed synthetic multi-turn
> conversations. All dialogue was generated from project-owned symbolic lesson
> plans by GPT-5.6 Luna and independently filtered by Claude Sonnet 4.6. UAE
> institutional guidance, classical adab-of-learning scholarship, tutoring
> research, and abstracted public-interaction research informed the shared
> behavior grammar; no raw transcript, copied source prose, personal data,
> held-out evaluation prompt, or human/expert gold conversation appears in the
> SFT files.

Do not call the dataset authentic Emirati dialogue, expert-approved religious
instruction, or partly real conversation data. Those claims are not supported
by the released records.
